💡 How the scenarios map:

Scenario A — Substitution / Decoration

The quiz replaces a paper test (Substitution). The leaderboard adds a game mechanic — but if you remove it, the quiz still exists and has the same learning value. The mechanics are decoration. There is no transformation of what students are doing with their thinking.

Scenario B — Modification / Transformation

The branching narrative redesigns the task around student agency (Modification). Remove the branching structure and you remove the learning activity itself — students can no longer make decisions and experience consequences. This is transformation: the task could not exist in this form without AI.

Scenario C — Redefinition / Transformation

Students designing a game for a younger year group is the Redefinition level. The act of designing requires deep content understanding — students must know the material well enough to create meaningful choices around it. This task was not feasible for most teachers to assign before AI. And the peer teaching dimension produces some of the highest learning impact of any instructional strategy.
