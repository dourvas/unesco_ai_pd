💡 What this challenge reveals:

Why "Adjust" is usually the right starting point

Simply accepting the RED flag ignores information that materially changes the picture. Simply overriding it erases a legitimate data point. Adding context preserves both the data and your professional knowledge — and gives your Head of Year what they need to make a good decision. This is exactly what "human in the loop" should mean in practice.

Why the system design is also a concern

An Early Warning System that cannot incorporate teacher knowledge is not a decision-support tool — it is a decision-making tool. The distinction matters. If this happens once, you manage it. If it happens for 30 students every Monday, individual case management becomes unsustainable, and the system's flaws compound. Raising this with leadership is a form of professional accountability, not just a personal concern.

The documentation habit

Whenever you override an AI output in a high-stakes situation, a brief written note protects you. Not a formal report — two sentences: what the AI flagged, why you judged differently, what you did instead. If a question is raised later, you can demonstrate that you exercised professional judgment, not that you ignored the system or acted without thought.
