PROODOS Practice Workshop — Moderation Policy

How content visibility, hiding, and author control work in the Practice Workshop.

{# ── TL;DR card ────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

TL;DR

  • Posts are visible immediately when you share them. No approval gate.
  • The researcher reviews content weekly (~30 min/week).
  • Posts may be hidden only for 4 specific reasons (listed below).
  • Posts are never hidden for pedagogical disagreement, quality, theory, or aesthetics.
  • You retain full control of your own posts and comments — edit, withdraw, delete at any time.
{# ── How moderation works ──────────────────────────────────── #}

How moderation works

Your post is visible to the community the moment you share it — there is no approval gate. We trust teachers to engage critically through comments rather than pre-approving each other's work.

A researcher can hide content after the fact, but only for the four specific reasons listed below. Hide actions are documented and reversible.

{# ── 4 hide-trigger criteria ───────────────────────────────── #}

Posts may be hidden for these 4 reasons only

If a researcher hides your post, it stops appearing in the public feed. You still see your own post (with a "currently hidden" notice) and can revise it; ask a researcher to restore it once revised.

1. Platform safety violation

Content that promotes harm to students, ignores child safeguarding, recommends illegal AI use cases, or proposes AI-only decisions on consequential outcomes (final grading, admission, special-needs placement) without human oversight.

2. Off-topic spam

Content not related to AI in education, promotional content for unrelated products/services, or duplicate/automated submissions.

3. Real student PII

Content that includes identifying student information (real names, photos, identifying anecdotes) that should be anonymised before sharing.

4. Copyright violation

Explicit infringement of third-party content (lyrics, full articles, copyrighted curricula, trademarked imagery).

{# ── 7 hide-NEVER criteria ────────────────────────────────── #}

Posts will never be hidden for these reasons

The following reflect community variance, not policy violations. If a hide action would be triggered by any of these rather than by the four trigger criteria above, the researcher does not hide. We trust the community to engage critically through comments instead.

  • Pedagogical disagreement
  • Quality concerns ("not good enough")
  • Different theoretical orientations
  • Beginner-level work
  • Subject-area unfamiliarity
  • Personal aesthetic preferences
  • Disagreement with the author's framing
{# ── Author self-service ──────────────────────────────────── #}

You retain full control of your own content

Researcher moderation is separate from author self-service. You can take any of these actions at any time, without researcher involvement:

Action What happens
Edit own post title Title updates instantly. Body (the canvas/lesson/unit content) stays the same — it was generated from your work.
Withdraw own post Post stops appearing in the feed. You still see it (with a "withdrawn" notice). You can ask a researcher to restore it.
Edit own comment Comment text updates. An "(edited)" marker appears for transparency.
Delete own comment Comment stops appearing on the post. The comment count decrements.
{# ── When the researcher reviews ──────────────────────────── #}

When the researcher reviews

The researcher reviews the Workshop weekly. If something needs to be hidden under one of the four criteria above, it usually happens within a week of being posted.
